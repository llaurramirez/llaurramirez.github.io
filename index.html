Dear Pinterest Team,

I'm applying for the Product Management Apprenticeship at Pinterest. I'm an internal auditor at Chewy, and for the last four years I've been doing product work without the title: scoping problems, prioritizing by risk and impact, and building the tools to prove it rather than simply recommending them.

Two projects show how I approach this work. I designed and led a compliance monitoring pilot that replaced semi-annual, sample-based safety audits across our warehouses with daily, real-time monitoring. It connects HR, training, and Slack data so site leaders are alerted the moment a high-risk safety course lapses, instead of waiting on the next audit cycle. I scoped the first version to the single highest-risk area on purpose, and built the framework so it could extend to equipment training and inventory controls next without a redesign. Separately, I noticed our SOX team's weekly meetings were spent reporting spreadsheet status instead of making decisions, so I built an interactive dashboard that forecasts thirteen weeks of workload, flags capacity risk before it happens, and surfaces which controls are ready to test early. My team now runs its weekly meetings on it. I've also led rollout of a generative AI tool that automated our audit reporting, cutting turnaround time by 80 percent. The harder part of that project wasn't building the tool; it was training my team to trust and adopt it, which is its own product problem.

What connects these projects is the judgment behind them: deciding what to prioritize, how far to scope something, and how to get people who don't report to me to act on what the data shows. That is the discipline I want to sharpen as a PM, under real mentorship and with real stakes.

Pinterest interests me because it is built for the moment before a decision gets made, when someone is still figuring out what they want. I have spent my career improving that same moment for people, just in a different domain, and I want to bring that instinct to a team building it at scale.

Thank you for considering my application. I would welcome the opportunity to bring my operational judgment, leadership experience, and drive to Pinterest.

Warmly,
